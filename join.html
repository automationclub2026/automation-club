Join the Industrial Automation Club

Become part of a student-led technical community focused on Industrial Automation, PLC systems, robotics, digital manufacturing, smart factories and practical engineering learning.

Membership

Who Can Join?

The club is open to motivated students, early-career engineers and technology enthusiasts who want to learn, build and collaborate in the field of automation.

Students

Engineering students from electrical, mechanical, mechatronics, computer science, industrial engineering and related fields.

Project Builders

Students who want to build real technical projects, contribute to research ideas and improve their engineering portfolio.

Beginners Welcome

No advanced experience is required. Members can start from basics and grow through workshops, mentoring and teamwork.

Process

How to Join

The joining process is simple and designed for students who want to actively participate.

Submit Your Interest

Share your name, university, study program and area of interest in automation.

Attend an Introduction Session

Meet the local chapter team, understand current projects and learn how the club works.

Select Your Activity Area

Choose between projects, workshops, research, documentation, event organization or chapter coordination.

Start Contributing

Join a project group, support chapter activities and build practical automation experience with the team.

Benefits

Why Join?

Hands-on Skills

Gain practical exposure to automation systems, PLC logic, sensors, robotics, digital twins and industrial workflows.

Team Projects

Work on structured projects that can be shown in your portfolio, CV, university presentations or research proposals.

Industry Mindset

Understand how real factories, warehouses, production lines and industrial systems are planned and improved.

Leadership

Take responsibility as a chapter volunteer, project coordinator, workshop organizer or technical documentation lead.

Membership Areas

Members can contribute in technical projects, research writing, workshops, chapter coordination, industrial visits, training sessions, documentation, website updates and student outreach.

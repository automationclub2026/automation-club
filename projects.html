Current project portfolio

Four focused projects created for students interested in automation, digital manufacturing, process optimization, and research-based engineering.

Digital Twin Active

Digital Twinning for Oil Refinery Operations

A research and simulation project focused on creating a digital representation of refinery process areas to understand equipment behavior, process flow, alarms, and operational performance.

FocusProcess Simulation
OutputDigital Model
  • Model refinery units such as tanks, pumps, valves, sensors, and pipelines.
  • Study process data, control logic, and alarm conditions.
  • Create a visual dashboard for monitoring simulated refinery performance.
PLC Audit Active

PLC-Based Automation Audit in Warehouse Systems

A practical improvement project to study warehouse automation processes and identify how PLC logic, sensor placement, interlocks, and operator workflow can reduce errors.

FocusError Reduction
OutputAudit Report
  • Map warehouse flow from receiving to sorting, storage, and dispatch.
  • Identify common automation failure points and manual process mistakes.
  • Recommend PLC logic improvements, sensor checks, and HMI warnings.
Smart Maintenance Active

Predictive Maintenance Research for Industrial Motors

A research project exploring how vibration, temperature, current, and runtime data can be used to predict motor faults before breakdowns occur in automation systems.

FocusCondition Monitoring
OutputResearch Prototype
  • Study motor fault indicators such as overheating, imbalance, and bearing wear.
  • Collect and compare sensor-based condition data.
  • Design a simple fault-warning concept for maintenance teams.
Automation Research Active

Energy-Efficient Automation for Smart Manufacturing

A research-oriented project studying how automated machines, conveyors, compressors, and production cells can reduce energy consumption without reducing productivity.

FocusEfficiency
OutputOptimization Study
  • Analyze energy usage patterns in automated production environments.
  • Study standby modes, smart scheduling, and load-based machine control.
  • Prepare improvement ideas for sustainable industrial automation.

How we work

Each project follows a simple engineering workflow so members can learn professionally and produce clean documentation.

1

Research

Understand the industrial problem, collect references, and define the technical objective.

2

Design

Create system architecture, logic flow, diagrams, and expected project deliverables.

3

Build

Develop simulations, prototypes, dashboards, audit sheets, or proof-of-concept models.

4

Present

Document results and present outcomes through reports, demos, and club updates.

Want to contribute?

Members can join as researchers, programmers, documentation contributors, design members, or project coordinators. No advanced experience is required — curiosity and consistency matter most.
